## ðŸ“Œ Sample: Design a Product

**Question:**
"Design a product for [target users] to [solve problem]."

**Answer Framework:**

1. **Users**: Identify primary and secondary personas, their demographics, context, and constraints.

2. **Jobs**: Define the job-to-be-done (functional, emotional, social) and pain points (current friction, unmet needs, desired outcomes).

3. **Core Loop**: Map the user journey (discovery â†’ onboarding â†’ core usage â†’ retention) and engagement loop (trigger â†’ action â†’ reward â†’ investment).

4. **Key Features**: List must-have features (core functionality, differentiators) and nice-to-have features (enhancements, future considerations).

5. **Tradeoffs**: Discuss design tradeoffs (simplicity vs power, speed vs quality) and business tradeoffs (user value vs revenue, short-term vs long-term).

6. **Success Metrics**: Define user metrics (adoption, engagement, satisfaction) and business metrics (revenue, growth, strategic value).

## ðŸŽ¯ Key Principles

- **User-centric**: Start with users and their jobs-to-be-done, not features
- **Iterative**: Design for MVP first, then enhance
- **Balanced**: Consider tradeoffs explicitly
- **Measurable**: Define success metrics upfront
