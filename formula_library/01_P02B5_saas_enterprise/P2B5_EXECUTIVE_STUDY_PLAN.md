### Why SaaS/Enterprise Metrics Matter

**Key Insight:** SaaS/Enterprise products are about **recurring revenue and customer success**. Metrics must capture both acquisition (new customers) and expansion (existing customers) to maximize ARR and NRR.

**Business Model Characteristics:**
- Recurring revenue: ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue), MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)
- Customer success: Product adoption drives retention and expansion
- Sales cycles: Longer, relationship-based
- Expansion revenue: Critical for growth (upsells, cross-sells)

### BUCKET 2: Revenue Metrics (ARR, NRR, MRR)
**Questions:** ~3 | **Priority:** ðŸŸ¢ GREEN (High-yield)

**Board Slide Bullets:**
- **ARR/MRR:** Annual/Monthly Recurring Revenue - predictable revenue base
- **NRR:** Net Revenue Retention = (Starting ARR + Expansion - Churn) / Starting ARR
- **Key Insight:** NRR > 100% means growing from existing customers (best growth)
- **Measurement:** ARR growth rate, NRR by cohort, expansion rate, churn rate

**Concrete Examples:**
- "ARR: Annual Recurring Revenue - predictable revenue base"
- "NRR: Net Revenue Retention - expansion minus churn, target > 100%"
- "Expansion rate: % of customers who increase spend - drives NRR"

**Representative Question Types (Do 5 only):**
- How do you measure ARR growth?
- What is NRR and why does it matter?
- How would you improve NRR?
- What's the relationship between ARR and NRR?
- How do you measure expansion revenue?

**â¤ï¸ Reusable Narrative (Base Story - Adapt for Each Question):**
> "For revenue metrics, I measure ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue) as the base, NRR (Net Revenue Retention) as the growth engine, and expansion rate as the driver. NRR = (Starting ARR + Expansion - Churn) / Starting ARR, target > 100%. Input KPIs: new customer ARR, expansion ARR (upsells, cross-sells), churn ARR. Leading indicators: expansion signal strength (feature usage, seat growth), time to expansion. Guardrails: churn rate (must be low), CAC payback (must be < 12 months), customer concentration (must be diversified). NRR > 100% means growing from existing customers, which is the best type of growth."

- How do you measure customer success?

Input KPIs: product engagement (sessions, features used), support ticket volume (lower is better), customer satisfaction (NPS). Leading indicators: first-feature-use rate, time-to-first-value, expansion signal strength. Guardrails: adoption depth (must use core features), support burden (must be manageable), customer satisfaction (must be positive). ### BUCKET 4: Acquisition & Unit Economics
**Questions:** ~2 | **Priority:** ðŸŸ¡ YELLOW

**Board Slide Bullets:**
- **Acquisition Metrics:** CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost), CAC payback period, LTV/CAC ratio
- **Unit Economics:** LTV (Lifetime Value), CAC, payback period, gross margin
- **Key Insight:** LTV/CAC > 3:1 is healthy, payback < 12 months is good
- **Measurement:** CAC by channel, LTV by cohort, payback period, gross margin

**Concrete Examples:**
- "CAC: Customer Acquisition Cost - cost to acquire one customer"
- "LTV/CAC ratio: Lifetime Value / CAC - target > 3:1"
- "Payback period: months to recover CAC - target < 12 months"

**Representative Question Types (Do 5 only):**
- How do you measure CAC?
- What is LTV/CAC ratio and why does it matter?
- How would you improve unit economics?
- What's the relationship between CAC and LTV?
- How do you measure payback period?

**â¤ï¸ Reusable Narrative (Base Story - Adapt for Each Question):**
> "For acquisition and unit economics, I measure CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost), LTV (Lifetime Value), and payback period. LTV/CAC ratio target > 3:1, payback period target < 12 months. Input KPIs: CAC by channel, LTV by cohort, gross margin, churn rate. Leading indicators: conversion rate by channel, time to value, expansion signals. Guardrails: CAC payback (must be < 12 months), LTV/CAC (must be > 3:1), gross margin (must be > 70%). Healthy unit economics enable sustainable growth."
