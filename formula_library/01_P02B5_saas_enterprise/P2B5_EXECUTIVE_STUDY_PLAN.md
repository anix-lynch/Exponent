### Why SaaS/Enterprise Metrics Matter

**Key Insight:** SaaS/Enterprise products are about **recurring revenue and customer success**. Metrics must capture both acquisition (new customers) and expansion (existing customers) to maximize ARR and NRR.

**Business Model Characteristics:**
- Recurring revenue: ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue), MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)
- Customer success: Product adoption drives retention and expansion
- Sales cycles: Longer, relationship-based
- Expansion revenue: Critical for growth (upsells, cross-sells)

### BUCKET 1: Define NSM for SaaS/Enterprise Products
**Questions:** ~4 | **Priority:** üü¢ GREEN (Master this)

**Board Slide Bullets:**
- **NSM Options:** ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue), NRR (Net Revenue Retention), Product Adoption Score
- **Key Insight:** NRR is often the NSM - it measures expansion minus churn (target > 100%)
- **Input KPIs:** Revenue (new customers, expansion, renewals), Product Adoption (feature usage, seat utilization), Customer Health (engagement, support tickets, NPS)
- **Leading Indicators:** Time to value, Feature adoption rate, Expansion signal strength
- **Guardrails:** Churn rate, CAC payback period, Customer concentration risk

**Concrete Examples:**
- "SaaS NSM: NRR (Net Revenue Retention) - expansion revenue minus churn, target > 100%"
- "Enterprise NSM: ARR Growth - new customers + expansion - churn"

### BUCKET 2: Revenue Metrics (ARR, NRR, MRR)
**Questions:** ~3 | **Priority:** üü¢ GREEN (High-yield)

**Board Slide Bullets:**
- **ARR/MRR:** Annual/Monthly Recurring Revenue - predictable revenue base
- **NRR:** Net Revenue Retention = (Starting ARR + Expansion - Churn) / Starting ARR
- **Key Insight:** NRR > 100% means growing from existing customers (best growth)
- **Measurement:** ARR growth rate, NRR by cohort, expansion rate, churn rate

**Concrete Examples:**
- "ARR: Annual Recurring Revenue - predictable revenue base"
- "NRR: Net Revenue Retention - expansion minus churn, target > 100%"
- "Expansion rate: % of customers who increase spend - drives NRR"

**Representative Question Types (Do 5 only):**
- How do you measure ARR growth?
- What is NRR and why does it matter?
- How would you improve NRR?
- What's the relationship between ARR and NRR?
- How do you measure expansion revenue?

**‚ù§Ô∏è Reusable Narrative (Base Story - Adapt for Each Question):**

**Framework:** `ARR ‚Üí NRR ‚Üí Expansion Rate ‚Üí Input KPIs ‚Üí Guardrails`

**Memorizable Answer:**

For revenue metrics, I measure three key metrics.

**1Ô∏è‚É£ ARR** ‚Üí Annual Recurring Revenue - predictable revenue base.

**2Ô∏è‚É£ NRR** ‚Üí Net Revenue Retention = (Starting ARR + Expansion - Churn) / Starting ARR (target > 100%).

**3Ô∏è‚É£ Expansion Rate** ‚Üí % of customers who increase spend - drives NRR.

**4Ô∏è‚É£ Input KPIs** ‚Üí New customer ARR, expansion ARR (upsells, cross-sells), churn ARR.

**5Ô∏è‚É£ Guardrails** ‚Üí Churn rate (must be low), CAC payback (must be < 12 months), customer concentration (must be diversified).

**Key Principle:** NRR > 100% means growing from existing customers - best type of growth.

### BUCKET 4: Acquisition & Unit Economics
**Questions:** ~2 | **Priority:** üü° YELLOW

**Board Slide Bullets:**
- **Acquisition Metrics:** CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost), CAC payback period, LTV/CAC ratio
- **Unit Economics:** LTV (Lifetime Value), CAC, payback period, gross margin
- **Key Insight:** LTV/CAC > 3:1 is healthy, payback < 12 months is good
- **Measurement:** CAC by channel, LTV by cohort, payback period, gross margin

**Concrete Examples:**
- "CAC: Customer Acquisition Cost - cost to acquire one customer"
- "LTV/CAC ratio: Lifetime Value / CAC - target > 3:1"
- "Payback period: months to recover CAC - target < 12 months"

**Representative Question Types (Do 5 only):**
- How do you measure CAC?
- What is LTV/CAC ratio and why does it matter?

**Memorizable Answer:**

For acquisition and unit economics, I measure three key metrics.

**1Ô∏è‚É£ CAC** ‚Üí Customer Acquisition Cost.
