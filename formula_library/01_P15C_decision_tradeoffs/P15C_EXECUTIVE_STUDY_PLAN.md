## üìä CONCEPT BUCKET BREAKDOWN

### BUCKET 1: Decision-Making & Tradeoffs Framework
**Questions:** ~15 | **Priority:** üü¢ GREEN (Master this)

**Board Slide Bullets:**
- **What:** "Tell me about a prioritization tradeoff" or "Time you had to say no" - decision-making questions
- **Framework:** Situation ‚Üí Task ‚Üí Action (Decision-Making Focus) ‚Üí Result (STAR)
- **Situation:** Context (What was the decision? When? Where?), Challenge (What was the tradeoff between options? What was the prioritization challenge? What made it difficult?)
- **Task:** Your responsibility (What was my responsibility?), Decision needed (What decision needed to be made?), Goal (What was the goal?)
- **Action:** Framework used (RICE, Cost/benefit, Impact/effort, etc.), Factors considered (User impact, Business impact, Technical feasibility, Risk, Timeline, Resources), Stakeholders consulted (Who I talked to, What input I gathered), Decision made (What I decided, Why this option), Why I chose this (My reasoning, Tradeoffs evaluated, Alternatives considered, Judgment)
- **Result:** Outcome (What happened? What was the result? Did the decision work?), Impact (What was the impact?), Learning (What did I learn? Focus on showing good judgment, not just process)

**Concrete Examples:**
- "Prioritization tradeoff: Situation (Decision needed, tradeoffs, context), Task (My role, goal), Action (How I approached - framework, factors, stakeholders, decision, reasoning), Result (Outcome, impact, learning)"
- "Say no: Situation (Request, context, constraints), Task (My role, goal), Action (How I handled - reasoning, communication, alternatives), Result (Outcome, impact, learning)"

**Representative Questions (Do 5 only):**
- Q1965: Tell me about a time when you made a prioritization tradeoff. (prioritization tradeoff angle)
- Q1948: Tell me about a time when you had to say no to someone. (saying no angle)
- Q1939: Tell me about a time when you had to make a decision quickly. (quick decision angle)
- Q1967: Tell me about a time when you made an important decision without your boss's approval. (independent decision angle)
- Q2007: Tell me about a time when you were forced to prioritize multiple projects. (multi-project prioritization angle)
