## üìä CONCEPT BUCKET BREAKDOWN

### BUCKET 1: Product Sense (Behavioral) Framework
**Questions:** ~35 | **Priority:** üü¢ GREEN (Master this)

**Board Slide Bullets:**
- **What:** "Tell me about a product you shipped" or "Product you're proud of" - behavioral product questions
- **Framework:** Situation ‚Üí Task ‚Üí Action ‚Üí Result (STAR)
- **Situation:** Context (What product/feature? When? Where?), Challenge (What was the context? What obstacles? What was at stake?)
- **Task:** Your role (What was your responsibility? What was I asked to do?), Goal (What were you trying to achieve? What constraints existed - time, resources, stakeholders?)
- **Action:** User research (What I learned about users), Product decisions (What I decided and why), Design choices (How I designed it), Launch strategy (How I launched it), Why this approach (Reasoning, alternatives considered)
- **Result:** Outcome (What happened? What was the result? Did I achieve my goal?), Impact (What was the impact? Quantify when possible - metrics, user impact, business impact), Learning (What did I learn? What would I do differently?)

**Concrete Examples:**
- "Product you shipped: Situation (Product X, launched Y, context Z), Task (My role, goal), Action (User research, decisions, design, launch), Result (Outcome, impact, learning)"
- "Feature you launched: Situation (Feature X, context), Task (My role, goal), Action (Approach, decisions), Result (Outcome, impact, learning)"

**Representative Questions (Do 5 only):**
- Q779: Explain a feature you've launched. (feature launch angle)
- Q154: Can you talk us through a product you've scoped and built? Include the goal and your specific role. (product case study angle)
- Q1806: Share your experience around launching a product from 0 to 1. (0-to-1 launch angle)
- Q1866: Tell me about a product or feature you shipped. (product shipping angle)
- Q804: Explain how you took or would take a product from ideation to launch. (ideation to launch angle)

**Memorizable Answer:**

**2Ô∏è‚É£ Task** ‚Üí What was my responsibility? What was I asked to do? What was my goal? What constraints existed (time, resources, stakeholders)?

**3Ô∏è‚É£ Action** ‚Üí I use 'I' statements, focus on my specific actions:
  - **User research:** What I learned about users

**4Ô∏è‚É£ Result** ‚Üí What was the result? Did I achieve my goal? What was the impact? (Quantify when possible - metrics, user impact, business impact). What did I learn? What would I do differently?
