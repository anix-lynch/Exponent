## üîç HOW TO IDENTIFY P2 (NSM + KPI LADDER) QUESTIONS

**Even when "NSM" or "KPI" isn't mentioned, look for these keywords/phrases:**

### Explicit Metrics Keywords:
- "metrics", "KPIs", "north star", "success metrics", "key metrics"
- "how to measure", "define success", "what metrics", "track"

### Implicit P2 Indicators:
- **Definition questions:** "what is the north star metric?", "define success for X", "what metrics matter?"
- **Design + metrics:** "design X and define success", "build X and measure success"
- **Measurement questions:** "how would you measure X?", "what metrics for Y?", "track success"
- **Goal setting:** "set goals for team", "define KPIs", "what metrics to focus on"
- **Success definition:** "how do you define success?", "what does success look like?"

### P2 vs P1 Distinction:
- **P2 (NSM + KPI):** "How would you measure success?" ‚Üí Focus: DEFINE metrics (NSM ‚Üí Input KPIs ‚Üí Guardrails)
- **P1 (Metric Drop):** "Orders down 25%" ‚Üí Focus: DIAGNOSE why metric dropped (segment ‚Üí hypothesize)

### P2 vs P3 Distinction:
- **P2 (NSM + KPI):** "What metrics for this product?" ‚Üí Focus: DEFINE what to measure
- **P3 (Funnel):** "How to improve conversion?" ‚Üí Focus: OPTIMIZE existing funnel steps

### Red Flags (NOT P2):
- "X is down 25%" ‚Üí P1 (Metric Drop)
- "Users drop at checkout" ‚Üí P3 (Funnel)
- "Cohort retention declined" ‚Üí P4 (Cohort/Retention)

## üìä CONCEPT BUCKET BREAKDOWN

### BUCKET 1: Define NSM for Existing Product
**Questions:** ~50 | **Priority:** üü¢ GREEN (Master this)

**Board Slide Bullets:**
- **What:** "What is the north star metric for [Product]?" or "Define success metrics for [Product]"
- **Framework:** NSM ‚Üí Input KPIs ‚Üí Leading Indicators ‚Üí Guardrails ‚Üí Dashboard
- **Key Insight:** NSM = single metric that captures user value + business value, actionable, predictive
- **Input KPIs:** What drives the NSM? (user behavior, supply/quality, monetization)
- **Guardrails:** What must NOT break? (trust, long-term health, cost/compliance)

**Concrete Examples:**
- "Airbnb NSM: Nights booked (user value = travel, business value = revenue)"
- "Instagram NSM: Daily active users who share (engagement + growth)"
- "Uber NSM: Completed rides per week (mobility + marketplace health)"

**Representative Questions (Do 5 only):**
- Q188: Define a north star metric for Airbnb.
- Q103: As the CEO of WhatsApp, what are the top 5 metrics you should focus on to measure the app's success?
- Q193: Define success for TikTok.
- Q33: As a Home Page PM at Netflix, how would you measure the success of your product?
- Q47: As a PM at Meta, what key metrics would you track for Meta's Workplace Chat?

**‚ù§Ô∏è Reusable Narrative (Base Story - Adapt for Each Question):**

**Framework:** `NSM ‚Üí Input KPIs ‚Üí Leading Indicators ‚Üí Guardrails ‚Üí Dashboard`

**Memorizable Answer:**

When defining success for a product, I start with the North Star Metric.

**1Ô∏è‚É£ Define NSM** ‚Üí Single metric that represents both user value and business value. Must be actionable (we can influence it), measurable (we can track it), and predictive (predicts long-term success).

**2Ô∏è‚É£ Build Input KPIs** ‚Üí What drives the NSM? User behavior, supply/quality, monetization.

**3Ô∏è‚É£ Add Leading Indicators** ‚Üí Early signals: short-term behaviors, activation signals.

**4Ô∏è‚É£ Set Guardrails** ‚Üí What must not break? User trust, long-term health, cost/compliance.

**5Ô∏è‚É£ Design Dashboard** ‚Üí NSM at top, Input KPIs weekly, Leading Indicators daily, Guardrails as alerts.

**Key Principle:** NSM connects user value to business value - it's the bridge between what users care about and what drives business.

  - "Input KPIs: videos created, watch time, shares"
  - "Leading indicators: first-video creation rate, time-to-first-video-view"
  - "Guardrails: content moderation, creator satisfaction, platform safety"

  - "NSM: weekly engaged viewers who find content ‚Üí discovery + retention"

**‚ù§Ô∏è Reusable Narrative (Base Story - Adapt for Each Question):**

**Framework:** `Define Success Metric ‚Üí Input KPIs ‚Üí Leading Indicators ‚Üí Guardrails ‚Üí Measure Incrementally`

**Memorizable Answer:**

When measuring feature or product success, I define a success metric that ties to business outcomes.

**1Ô∏è‚É£ Define Success Metric** ‚Üí For new feature: adoption (weekly active users) OR engagement (feature usage per user).

**2Ô∏è‚É£ Identify Input KPIs** ‚Üí What drives success? Discovery rate, activation rate, retention.

**Key Principle:** Tie feature metrics to business outcomes - adoption and engagement must drive business value.

### BUCKET 4: Goal Setting & Team Metrics
**Questions:** ~30 | **Priority:** üü° YELLOW (High-yield but needs practice)

**Board Slide Bullets:**
- **What:** "How would you set goals for the team?" or "What metrics would you track for [Team/Product]?"
- **Framework:** Align team goals to NSM, break down into Input KPIs, set targets
- **Key Insight:** Goals should be tied to NSM, measurable, time-bound, and team-aligned
- **Approach:** Define team's contribution to NSM ‚Üí Set goal ‚Üí Break into Input KPIs ‚Üí Track progress

**Concrete Examples:**
- "Instagram Stories team goal: Increase Stories creation by 20% (drives NSM: DAU)"
- "Netflix recommendation team goal: Increase recommendation CTR by 15% (drives NSM: watch time)"

**Representative Questions (Do 5 only):**
- Q63: As a PM for Instagram stories, how would you set the goal for the team?
- Q42: As a PM at DoorDash, define some goals and metrics for the business.
- Q116: As the PM for Instagram Reels, how would you set goals for monetization?
- Q88: As a product manager, what KPIs matter most to you?
- Q69: As a PM for Spotify Podcasts, what success metrics would you define? If engagement on Spotify Music is down while engagement on Spotify Podcasts is up, what actions would you take?

**‚ù§Ô∏è Reusable Narrative (Base Story - Adapt for Each Question):**

**Framework:** `Align to NSM ‚Üí Define Team Influence ‚Üí Set Goal ‚Üí Break Down ‚Üí Set Guardrails`

**Memorizable Answer:**

When setting team goals, I align them to the product's NSM.

**1Ô∏è‚É£ Align to NSM** ‚Üí Team goals must drive the NSM.

**2Ô∏è‚É£ Define Team Influence** ‚Üí What can the team directly influence? Usually an Input KPI that drives the NSM.

**3Ô∏è‚É£ Set Goal** ‚Üí Specific, measurable, time-bound (e.g., increase X by Y% in Z months).

**4Ô∏è‚É£ Break Down** ‚Üí Leading indicators the team can track daily.

**5Ô∏è‚É£ Set Guardrails** ‚Üí Ensure we don't optimize for the goal at the expense of other important metrics.

**Key Principle:** Goals connect team work to the bigger picture - communicate how team's work drives NSM.

---

**How to Adapt This Narrative for Each Question:**

- **Q63 (Instagram Stories team goal):** Focus on feature team
  - "Goal: increase Stories creation by 20% in Q1 ‚Üí drives NSM (DAU)"
  - "Breakdown: increase first-Story-creation rate by 15%, Stories-per-user by 10%, improve Stories discovery"
  - "Leading indicators: first-Story-creation rate, Stories views, Stories replies"
  - "Guardrails: feed engagement doesn't drop, user satisfaction maintained"

- **Q42 (DoorDash business goals):** Emphasize business-level
  - "Goals align to NSM (successful deliveries): 1) Supply: 10K active restaurants by end of year, 2) Demand: 2 orders per user per month, 3) Efficiency: 30-min average delivery time, 4) Quality: 95% order accuracy"
  - "Each goal has Input KPIs and leading indicators"
  - "Guardrails: restaurant satisfaction, delivery partner earnings, customer satisfaction"

- **Q116 (Instagram Reels monetization goals):** Focus on revenue
  - "Goal: increase ad revenue by 25% in 6 months ‚Üí drives business NSM (revenue)"
  - "Breakdown: increase ad impressions by 20%, improve ad CTR by 10%, increase advertiser spend"
  - "Leading indicators: ad load, skip rate, advertiser retention"
  - "Guardrails: user watch time doesn't drop, creator revenue share maintained, ad quality standards"

- **Q88 (KPIs that matter most):** Emphasize prioritization
  - "KPIs that matter: 1) NSM (outcome), 2) Input KPIs (what we control), 3) Leading Indicators (early signals), 4) Guardrails (what must not break)"
  - "Prioritize by: impact on NSM, controllability, predictive power"
  - "Focus on 3-5 key metrics, not 20+"

- **Q69 (Spotify Podcasts metrics + cannibalization):** Focus on tradeoff analysis
  - "NSM: weekly engaged podcast listeners"
  - "Input KPIs: podcast episodes consumed, podcast discovery rate"
  - "If Music down while Podcasts up ‚Üí investigate: cannibalization or different segments?"
  - "Segment: podcast-only, music-only, both"
  - "If cannibalization ‚Üí optimize for total engagement (Music + Podcasts) as true NSM"
  - "Actions: improve Music recommendations, create Music-Podcast playlists, ensure both surfaces get equal promotion"
