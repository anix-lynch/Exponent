### Why Marketplace Metrics Matter

**Key Insight:** Marketplaces are about **connecting supply and demand**. Metrics must capture both sides (supply = sellers/listings, demand = buyers/searches) and the health of transactions between them.

**Business Model Characteristics:**
- Two-sided: supply (sellers/listings) + demand (buyers/searches)
- Transaction-based: revenue from successful matches
- Liquidity: need enough supply and demand to match
- Trust: both sides need to trust the platform

### BUCKET 2: Supply/Demand Balance Metrics
**Questions:** ~2 | **Priority:** ğŸŸ¢ GREEN (High-yield)

**Board Slide Bullets:**
- **Supply Metrics:** Active listings, listing quality score, seller satisfaction, seller retention
- **Demand Metrics:** Search volume, booking conversion, repeat bookings, user retention
- **Key Insight:** Balance is critical - too much supply = low prices, too little = low conversion
- **Measurement:** Supply/demand ratio, booking conversion rate, listing utilization

**Concrete Examples:**
- "Supply/demand ratio: active listings / searches - optimal around 1.2-1.5"
- "Booking conversion: bookings / searches - measures demand quality"
- "Listing utilization: bookings / listings - measures supply efficiency"

**Representative Question Types (Do 5 only):**
- How do you measure supply/demand balance?
- What metrics indicate healthy marketplace?
- How would you improve listing quality?
- What's the relationship between supply and conversion?
- How do you prevent seller churn?

**â¤ï¸ Reusable Narrative (Base Story - Adapt for Each Question):**
> "For supply/demand balance, I measure the ratio of active listings to searches (optimal 1.2-1.5), booking conversion rate (bookings/searches, target > 10%), and listing utilization (bookings/listings, target > 20%). Input KPIs: supply (active listings, listing quality, seller satisfaction), demand (search volume, user retention, repeat bookings). Leading indicators: new listing rate, search-to-booking conversion, seller onboarding completion. Guardrails: listing quality (maintain standards), seller satisfaction (prevent churn), price competitiveness (stay competitive)."
