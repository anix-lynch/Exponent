### BUCKET 1: Market Analysis Framework
**Questions:** ~10 | **Priority:** ðŸŸ¢ GREEN (Master this)

**Board Slide Bullets:**
- **What:** "What are the competitors?" or "How would you disrupt X?" - core market analysis framework
- **Framework:** Competitors â†’ Differentiation â†’ Market Conditions â†’ Strategy
- **Competitors:** Direct (Same user, same job), Indirect (Different product, same job), Status quo (Excel, email, manual work, doing nothing), Rule: The real competitor is often the status quo
- **Differentiation:** Price, Quality/Performance, Convenience/UX, Trust/Brand, Ecosystem/Lock-in, Rule: Features don't differentiate â€” reasons do
- **Market Conditions:** Market size & growth, Buyer vs seller power, Switching costs, Regulation/risk, Network effects (yes/no)
- **Strategy:** Compete head-on, Focus on niche, Undercut on cost, Differentiate on UX/trust, Bundle/unbundle

**Concrete Examples:**
- "Food delivery competitors: Direct (DoorDash, Uber Eats), Indirect (Restaurant websites), Status quo (Cooking at home), Differentiation (Speed vs price vs selection), Market (High competition, thin margins, network effects), Strategy (Focus on reliability for repeat users)"

**â¤ï¸ Reusable Narrative (Base Story - Adapt for Each Question):**
> "When analyzing markets and competition, I use Competitors â†’ Differentiation â†’ Market Conditions â†’ Strategy. Price (Cheaper option), Quality/Performance (Better quality, faster, more reliable), Convenience/UX (Easier to use, better experience), Trust/Brand (Trusted brand, reputation), Ecosystem/Lock-in (Integrated with other products, switching costs).
